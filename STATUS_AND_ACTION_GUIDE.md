# STATUS & ACTION Tags - Complete Guide

## Overview
- **STATUS** = Your **current competitive position** (where you stand vs competitors)
- **ACTION** = **Optimization recommendation** (what to do based on performance + settings)

## üéØ ACTION Definitions (Optimization Recommendations)

### **INCREASE** ‚Üë
- **Meaning**: **Increase investment** in this channel
- **When Recommended**:
  - You're BEHIND or CRITICAL (status)
  - Gap is significant (negative gap below threshold)
  - Competitive Index is below threshold
  - Channel is NOT protected (not in top X% by threshold setting)
- **Translation**: "We're losing here - invest more to catch up"

### **MAINTAIN** ‚Äî
- **Meaning**: **Keep current investment level**
- **When Recommended**:
  - Channel is **PROTECTED** (in top X% performers by threshold)
  - OR Competitive Index ‚â• 100 (LEADING or DOMINANT)
  - OR Stable performance (default for channels that don't need changes)
- **Translation**: "Things are good - don't change anything"

### **ADD** +
- **Meaning**: **Add this channel** to your media plan
- **When Recommended**:
  - STATUS = OPPORTUNITY (you're absent, competitors are present meaningfully)
  - Competitor Reach ‚â• 2% AND Channel Share ‚â• 1%
- **Translation**: "We're missing out - start advertising here"

### **DECREASE** ‚Üì
- **Meaning**: **Reduce investment** (reallocate budget elsewhere)
- **When Recommended**:
  - Low reach (< 1.5%) but over-indexed (Index > 120)
  - Channel is NOT protected
  - You're doing well but spending too much for the return
- **Translation**: "We're over-investing here - move budget to better channels"

## üîÑ Common STATUS + ACTION Combinations Explained

### **CLOSE + MAINTAIN**
- **What it means**: 
  - STATUS: You're neck-and-neck with competitors (Index 80-99)
  - ACTION: Keep current investment
- **Why MAINTAIN?**: 
  - Channel is likely **PROTECTED** (in top performers)
  - OR Performance is stable enough that changes aren't needed
- **Business Logic**: "We're competitive here, and it's working - don't rock the boat"

### **LEADING + MAINTAIN**
- **What it means**:
  - STATUS: You're ahead (Index ‚â• 100)
  - ACTION: Keep current investment
- **Why MAINTAIN?**: 
  - You're winning, so maintain the winning strategy
- **Business Logic**: "If it ain't broke, don't fix it"

### **BEHIND + INCREASE**
- **What it means**:
  - STATUS: Competitors are ahead (Index 50-79)
  - ACTION: Increase investment
- **Why INCREASE?**: 
  - You're losing ground, need to catch up
  - Gap is significant enough to warrant more investment
- **Business Logic**: "We're losing - invest more to compete"

### **CRITICAL + INCREASE**
- **What it means**:
  - STATUS: You're way behind (Index < 50)
  - ACTION: Increase investment
- **Why INCREASE?**: 
  - Urgent situation - significant underperformance
  - High priority to catch up
- **Business Logic**: "We're getting crushed - urgent investment needed"

### **OPPORTUNITY + ADD**
- **What it means**:
  - STATUS: You're absent, competitors are present meaningfully
  - ACTION: Add this channel
- **Why ADD?**: 
  - Clear opportunity to enter a channel where competitors are succeeding
- **Business Logic**: "We're missing out - start advertising here"

### **MONOPOLY + MAINTAIN**
- **What it means**:
  - STATUS: You own this channel (no competition)
  - ACTION: Keep current investment
- **Why MAINTAIN?**: 
  - You're winning without competition - maintain position
- **Business Logic**: "We own this - keep doing what we're doing"

### **LEADING + DECREASE** (Rare)
- **What it means**:
  - STATUS: You're ahead (Index ‚â• 100)
  - ACTION: Reduce investment
- **Why DECREASE?**: 
  - Low reach (< 1.5%) but over-indexed (Index > 120)
  - You're winning but spending too much for the return
- **Business Logic**: "We're winning easily - we can reduce spend and still win"

## ‚öôÔ∏è How Optimization Settings Affect Actions

### **Intensity Setting** (5-30%)
- **Low Intensity (5-10%)**: Conservative - only recommends changes for big gaps
- **Medium Intensity (11-20%)**: Balanced - recommends changes for moderate gaps
- **High Intensity (21-30%)**: Aggressive - recommends changes for smaller gaps

### **Threshold Setting** (50-90%)
- **What it does**: Protects top X% of channels by reach
- **Example**: Threshold = 70% means top 70% of channels are "protected"
- **Protected channels**: Usually get MAINTAIN recommendation (unless critically underperforming)

## üí° Key Takeaways

1. **STATUS = Current Situation** (where you are now)
2. **ACTION = Recommendation** (what to do about it)
3. **They can seem contradictory** (e.g., CLOSE + MAINTAIN) because:
   - STATUS looks at competitive position
   - ACTION considers protection, performance, and optimization settings
4. **PROTECTED channels** (top performers) usually get MAINTAIN even if STATUS is CLOSE/BEHIND
5. **The REASON column** explains why each ACTION was recommended
